# Human Rights Engine · 人权引擎

A civilisation-scale, bilingual (English + 中文) exploration of human rights, dignity, freedom, personhood, justice, power, surveillance and AI ethics. It reads rights not as slogans but as civilisation's standing answer to one permanent danger — that the powerful will treat the powerless as material. A right is a place where power is told *no* in advance, and the arc of dignity is the slow widening of the circle of beings to whom that *no* applies.

Ten interlocking systems — the origin of rights, dignity & personhood, freedom & the limits of power, law & constitutions, slavery & oppression, economic & social rights, surveillance & digital rights, AI & future rights, universalism vs cultural difference, and a unified model — each with hand-built, fully interactive bilingual visualizations. A live `EN | 中文` toggle switches every word without a reload.

## Interactive visualizations

- **Rights Timeline** — the evolution of rights from tribal custom to the Universal Declaration, with the "circle of who counts" widening across history.
- **Moral Circle** — an expanding-rings model of dignity, from self & kin to all humans, sentient animals, future generations and synthetic minds.
- **Freedom × Power Map** — governance archetypes plotted on a liberty × order plane.
- **Constitution Lab** — an orbital diagram of how institutions check a central executive; toggle "remove the checks" to watch power expand.
- **Oppression Timeline** — a grave, victim-forward, public-source catalog of slavery, serfdom, caste, colonialism, genocide and political repression, and how each was undone.
- **Rights Ladder** — a fair comparator of liberal, socialist, communitarian and hybrid approaches to what a right even is.
- **Surveillance Lab** — a digital-rights dashboard with a live "chilling effect" crowd simulation.
- **AI Rights Map** — a moral-status ladder with a draggable "draw your line" threshold.
- **Universalism Map** — four dual-pole value sliders and the universal floor beneath cultural difference.
- **Rights Stability Model** — the 7-axis meta-model radar with live sliders and epoch presets.
- **Recursive Rights Engine** — runs the seven forces forward across eight epochs (tribal → empire → constitutional → industrial → digital → AI → synthetic → planetary).
- **Rights Analyst** — ask a question and hear it answered through six scholarly lenses at once: philosopher, constitutional scholar, historian, ethicist, political theorist and AI-governance analyst.

## Meta-model

`Human Rights Stability = Dignity Recognition + Institutional Constraints + Legal Protection + Freedom Preservation + Economic Participation + Information Autonomy + Protection from Arbitrary Power`
